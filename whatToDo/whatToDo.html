Jose Guadalupe Posada Museum

Posada was an artist and caricaturist whose art was often combined with socio-political criticism. He is probably best known for his calacas and calaveras, the skeletons whose dark humor delighted the public. In his museum, you can see it's more than three thousand exhibits.

The museum is next to the Encino Church. You can attend engraving classes or enjoy a cultural event on Sunday at midday.

Lado norte Jardín del Encino s/n, Barrio Del Encino, 20240

Museo Nacional de la Muerte

The museum shown hundreds of death-related artifacts. It also includes the direction of depicting the historic role of death's iconography and the funereal arts within Mexican culture, both contemporary and ancient. The Museum curators conveyed the wide range of Mexican society's relationship towards death and Santa Muerte himself, ranging from the popular portrayal of a people who are always joking and laughing about mortality, to the nitty gritty — sometimes quite graphic — reality of death most corporeal from the pre-hispanic era to pop culture.

Rivero y Gutiérrez x, José María Morelos y Pavón, Zona Centro, 20000

Ferrocarrilero Aguascalientes

The museum is housed in the old loading depot built at the end of the nineteenth century and used to weigh and distribute a wide range of goods transported on the railway.

Today the museum has a collection of more than ten thousand pieces related to railway infrastructure, including machinery, tools, equipment and documents. The goal is to tell the story and display the history of the national railway system and its economic, political, social and cultural impact on the development of Aguascalientes in the early twentieth century.

Plaza de las Tres Centurias, 28 de Agosto S/N, Barrio de la Estación, 20259

Regional Museum of History

The museum tells the story of Aguascalientes and its people in the seven exhibition rooms where the past is recreated in an attempt to show how it was lived at the time.

The museum has a wide range of displays, including a fossil collection, pieces related to pre-Hispanic groups and their religious beliefs, and to the Chichimeca wars fought in the region.

Venustiano Carranza 118, Zona Centro, 20000

Centro Cultural los Arquitos

Los Arquitos is a hub of cultural activity in Aguascalientes and holds exhibitions, workshops, talks and concerts.

The Cultural Center was opened on November 12, 1994 at the site of the first public baths, which distributed water from Ojocaliente to fill the thirteen bathtubs and the sinks for washing clothes.

Alameda S/N, Barrio de la Purísima, 20000

El Caracol

El Caracol recreates different geological eras. As well, it counts with a water supply system that once conveyed water to the city of Aguascalientes. The water deposit has been preserved as well as a section of the subterranean channels which can be explored at a depth of twenty-three feet.

Other attractions include life-size figures of fauna from previous eras, ninety-million-year-old fossils, and the opportunity to discover local legends.

Càncer 546, Desarrollo Especial Parque Ecológico el Cedazo

Jardin de San Marcos

Since 1831, the gardens have been a center of social life and recreation. They are adorned with wrought-iron benches, a fountain below the gazebo, sculptures representing local customs and traditions and four entrances facing each of the cardinal points.

Every year the area is home to the San Marcos National Fair.

Jesús F. Contreras, Barrio de San Marcos, 20070

Andador J. Pani

Located in the traditional neighborhood of San Marcos, this pedestrianized streets is one of the liveliest areas of the San Marcos Fair in April.

With its traditional outdoor restaurants, known as merenderos, its Casino and cock-fighting arena, the walkway is a unique experience for all who stroll along its cobblestone route.

Locals and visitors come here all year round for a great atmosphere and fun.

José F. Elizondo 105, Desarrollo Especial Zona de la Feria

Isla San Marcos

Isla San Marcos sits on 109 acres and is one of the main venues for the San Marcos National Fair.

Principal attractions include the large artificial lake with fountains in the middle; the largest mechanical clock in Latin America, measuring nearly 89 feet in diameter; a zip line; the Catrina Monument in honor of José Guadalupe Posada, and the countless green areas.

Jesús F. Contreras, Barrio de San Marcos, 20070

Plaza Patria

La Plaza Patria is at the center of the city's life and has witnessed historic changes in Aguascalientes. In the center of the plaza is the Exedra, a monument erected in 1808 in honor of King Charles IV of Spain.

In this area, you can find the town hall, the cathedral, the first theatre, and a new renovated area that counts with regional art.

Zona Centro, 20000

Boca de Tunel

A circuit including 13 suspended bridges (the longest of which is 345-feet long at a height of 50 feet) and two zip lines that cross the ravine to the Potrerillos Dam, you'll never find yourself short on thrills or gorgeous natural settings.

After you've flown across the 140-meter-long zip line through a canyon, you can hire a local guide to take you down to the bottom of the dam while listening to the songs of the mockingbirds.

Calle Nueva Boca de Tunel

Malpaso

Located in the Sierra del Laurel, Malpaso Canyon is a major attraction, comprising a series of 160-foot elevations, from where you can see a wide variety of birds and contemplate a pretty landscape full of cacti and wild bushes.
